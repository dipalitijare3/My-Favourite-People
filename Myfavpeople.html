My Favourite People

Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, whose full name was Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, stands as one of India’s most revered scientists and leaders. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, into a humble family, he overcame early financial hardships through determination and hard work. Kalam’s academic journey began at St. Joseph’s College, Tiruchirappalli, where he studied Physics, followed by Aeronautical Engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology. Kalam’s career began at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) in 1958, and he later joined the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). At ISRO, he played a pivotal role in developing India’s first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-3), which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in 1980, marking a major milestone in India’s space program. His leadership in missile technology, particularly with the Agni and Prithvi missiles, earned him the title “Missile Man of India”. He was instrumental in advancing India’s nuclear capabilities, notably during the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998

Swami Vivekanand

Swami Vivekananda (1863–1902), born as Narendranath Datta in Kolkata, was a pioneering Hindu monk, philosopher, and social reformer who played a crucial role in introducing Vedanta and Yoga to the Western world. A brilliant student with a deep interest in spirituality, he became the chief disciple of the mystic Ramakrishna Paramahamsa, whose teachings inspired him to pursue a life of renunciation and service. After his guru’s death, Vivekananda traveled throughout India as a wandering monk, witnessing the poverty and hardships faced by the masses. This experience convinced him that spiritual awakening must be accompanied by social upliftment. In 1893, he gained international fame with his powerful speech at the Parliament of the World's Religions in Chicago, where he promoted religious tolerance and universal acceptance, beginning with the iconic words, “Sisters and brothers of America…”. His address left a lasting impression, establishing him as a major advocate for interfaith harmony and the dignity of all religions. Vivekananda’s teachings emphasized the potential divinity of every individual, self-realization, and service to humanity. He founded the Ramakrishna Mission, which continues to serve society through education, healthcare, and relief work. Swami Vivekananda passed away at the age of 39, but his legacy endures, inspiring millions to strive for personal excellence and social responsibility.

Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar

Dr. Bhimrao Ramji Ambedkar, widely known as Babasaheb Ambedkar, was a pioneering Indian jurist, economist, social reformer, and the chief architect of the Indian Constitution. Born on April 14, 1891, in Mhow, Madhya Pradesh, into a Dalit family, he faced intense caste-based discrimination from an early age but excelled academically, earning doctorates in economics from Columbia University and the London School of Economics. Ambedkar dedicated his life to the upliftment of marginalized communities, especially Dalits, and fought relentlessly to eradicate untouchability and caste-based injustices. He founded organizations such as the Bahishkrit Hitkarini Sabha and led movements for temple entry and equal rights, emphasizing the transformative power of education. As the Chairman of the Drafting Committee, Ambedkar played a pivotal role in framing the Indian Constitution, embedding principles of equality, justice, and fundamental rights for all citizens. Beyond his political and legal achievements, Ambedkar inspired millions by converting to Buddhism in 1956, sparking a significant socio-religious movement. He served as India’s first Law Minister and was posthumously awarded the Bharat Ratna in 1990 for his immense contributions. Dr. Ambedkar remains a symbol of social justice, empowerment, and the enduring fight for equality in India.

Sunita Williams

Sunita Williams is a renowned American astronaut and retired U.S. Navy officer, celebrated for her groundbreaking achievements in space exploration. Born on September 19, 1965, in Euclid, Ohio, to an Indian-American father and a Slovenian mother, Williams grew up in Massachusetts and developed a passion for science and aviation early in life. She graduated from the U.S. Naval Academy in 1987 and later earned a master’s degree in engineering management from the Florida Institute of Technology. Williams began her career as a helicopter pilot in the U.S. Navy, serving in the Persian Gulf and participating in humanitarian missions. Selected by NASA in 1998, she became an astronaut and embarked on her first space mission in 2006 aboard the Space Shuttle Discovery, spending 195 days on the International Space Station (ISS) and setting a record for the longest single spaceflight by a woman at that time. She is also famous for running the Boston Marathon on a treadmill in space. Over three space missions, Williams has accumulated more than 322 days in space and performed nine spacewalks, totaling over 62 hours—the most by any female astronaut. Her leadership was recognized when she twice served as ISS commander. Williams is admired for her technical skills, resilience, and for inspiring young people worldwide, especially girls, to pursue careers in science and space.

Sushmita Sen

Sushmita Sen is a celebrated Indian actress, model, and the first Indian woman to win the Miss Universe crown in 1994, achieving this milestone at just 18 years old. Born on November 19, 1975, in Hyderabad to a Bengali family, her father was a former Indian Air Force Wing Commander and her mother a jewelry designer. Sushmita attended Air Force Golden Jubilee Institute in New Delhi and St. Ann’s High School in Secunderabad, but chose not to pursue higher education, entering the Femina Miss India pageant instead. Her Miss Universe victory in Manila was historic, as she impressed judges with her poise, intelligence, and a memorable answer about the essence of being a woman, emphasizing motherhood and compassion. This win made her a national icon and inspired countless young women across India. After her pageant success, Sushmita transitioned to Bollywood, debuting in the film "Dastak" (1996) and later earning acclaim for roles in movies like "Biwi No.1" and "Main Hoon Na". She is also known for her philanthropic work and for being a single mother to two adopted daughters, Renee and Alisah. Sushmita Sen remains a symbol of empowerment, resilience, and elegance in Indian society.
